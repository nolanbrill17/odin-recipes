Rice recipe

Really good rice is both more complicated and more simple than it seems, but either way you slice it, it all starts with buying good rice. For short grain rice, I really like the Botan rice, but you can definitely find better rice out there.

What makes rice really good (and simplifies the process) is buying a rice cooker. They aren't expensive these days and are easy to find. More importantly they make the best rice you'll ever have and they're very easy to operate. Buy one here at this link if you don't have one

Ingredients

  • 75g of Botan short grain rice
  • 100g of water
  • literal pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Begin by washing the rice until the water runs clear. I usually rince the rice about 4-5 times before I feel good about it
  2. Add the water to the rice in the rice cooker pot. Follow the rice water/rice ratio. Add a pinch of salt.
  3. Make sure the rice cooker pot is in the rice cooker. Turn it on. Once it clicks into warm, remove the rice and fluff it.
